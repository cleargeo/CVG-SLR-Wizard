🌊 NOAA SLR Scenarios

Sweet et al. 2022 — NOAA Interagency Technical Report. Global mean sea level rise by 2100 relative to 2000 baseline. Five scenarios spanning the full planning probability range.

By 2100. Low probability, high-confidence minimum. Conservative baseline for long-lived infrastructure.
INT-LOW
+0.5 m
By 2100. Moderate mitigation scenario. Lower bound for most state and federal planning standards.
INTERMEDIATE

INT-HIGH

⚙️ How It Works

Three steps from project scope to downloadable inundation rasters — typically complete in minutes.

1
Configure Your Analysis
Submit your AOI (GeoJSON, WKT, or bounding box), select SLR scenarios, target horizon years, DEM source, and output resolution via REST API or JSON payload.
2
Engine Processes
The SLR engine retrieves the NOAA curve for your nearest tide gauge, applies the projected MSL offset to local datum, performs DEM differencing, and clips to your AOI.
3
Download & Deliver
Retrieve COG GeoTIFFs with embedded CRS (EPSG:4326), NAVD88 vertical datum, and structured JSON metadata. Import directly into ArcGIS, QGIS, or your web mapping platform.
